FAQ's

Q: What is Trauma?

A trauma is a stressful event or incident of a threatening nature, which is likely to have a pervasive impact on anyone experiencing it. Examples include events like serious accidents, rape, crime, natural and man-made disasters, witnessing a violent death, etc. Less dramatic events and incidents can have a similar effect if they exceed a person's capacity to cope, or if they are perceived by the person as a threat to their physical or psychological integrity.

Q: What is High Acuity ?

High Acuity Trauma case that requires special and frequent supervision or full physical assistance.

Q:What is EMT?

EMT term refers to the Emergency Medical Technicians.

Q: What is the Trauma app?

It is instant Reporting Emergency cases appdesigned for Android Mobile tablets. This app gives the Emergency Medical Technicians (EMTs) the facility to report new emergency cases (Trauma and High Acuity) while they are travelling to the nearest hospital.

Q: What is the Role of EMT user?

EMT user is the system user who is responsible for the reporting the Trauma and High Acuity cases using the mobile application.

Q: What is the Role of Clinical Back End user?

Clinical back end user is doctor in the hospital who is responsible for reviewing the reported cases from the Clinical back end panel. Clinical user is responsible for updating the case status when the case arrives to the hospital and when the treatment for the case is done.

Q: What is the Role of the System Administrator?

The system administrator is the responsible role for managing the system user and all the cases recorded in the system.

Q: Does Trauma app for Android tablet work with any version of OS?

You'll need Android OS 2.3 or a later version to use this app.

Q: How do I log in to the app?

You can access the login screen after launching the app from its icon. You'll be asked to log in with your user name and password. If you don't have account you need to sign up and create.

Q: Can I manage my profile from the app?

Yes, you can update your password, profile picture, and your address by selecting "Update profile" option.

Q: As an EMT user, how can I report the Trauma case?

You can login the application, select the type of trauma case you need to report whether it is Trauma 1 or Trauma 2 and then follow the wizard steps to report the details of the case.

Q: As an EMT user, how can I identify the injury location and type?

You can identify the injury location and type by pointing to the specified body part on the whole body image and select the injury type from the list.

Q: As an EMT user, can I view all the cases I reported?

Yes, you can view all the reported cases by yourself from "My History" option under settings icon.

Q: As an EMT user, can I update the case I have reported?

Yes, you can update the case when its status "not arrived". If the case status changes to be "Arrived" or "Finished" then you will not be able to update the case details.

Q: As a clinical back end user, can I update the case details I have received in the portal?

As a clinical user, you have the ability to update the reported cases status only and you don't have the facility to update the details of the case.

Q: Who has the authority to control my access to the application?

The system admin has the authority to manage the system users. The system admin can add, delete, block, and activate blocked users in the system.
